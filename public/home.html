What is the Pomodoro technique?

A study method built around focused sprints and planned breaks.

You pick one task, set a timer, work until it rings, then take a short break. After a few rounds, you take a longer one. That pattern helps you stay present instead of trying to push through hours without stopping.

Why it helps

It lowers the pressure to start.

A single 25 minute block feels manageable. That makes it easier to begin reading, writing, revising, or solving problems even when the task feels heavy.

What changes

You keep your attention longer.

Short breaks give your brain a reset before your focus drops too far. Over time, that usually means better concentration and less drift between tabs, notes, and distractions.

A simple way to use it

1

Choose one goal

Write a clear target for the session, like finishing one chapter or one exercise set.

2

Work until the timer ends

Keep the task narrow and avoid switching context unless it is necessary.

3

Take the break seriously

Stand up, stretch, drink water, then come back for the next round with fresh attention.
